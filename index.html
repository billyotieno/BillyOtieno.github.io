What would be the best place to Launch an New Restaurant in Nairobi City? What factors influence the restaurant set up?

Introduction

Kenya's capital city, Nairobi, is the most dynamic city in East Africa, also known as "green city in the sun". Its kenya's largest city and has a population of nearly 4 million. As the largest center of business and trade in East Africa and a regional location of various international companies and organizations, Nairobi has amassed a lot of interests from investors looking to launch restaurants, hotel, lodges and budget accommodations but the big question is: What would be the best location to set up a restaurant that is likely to guarantee a return on investment within Nairobi? and What are the major factors that contribute to the setup of restaurants in a particular location within Nairobi Town?

Data

The Data to come up the answers to this business problem was scraped from wikipedia, a list of location and sub-locations within Nairobi City. Using this data I extracted the latitude and longitudes then use Foursquare API to get the venues within each location and created a geographical mapping of all the restaurant within Nairobi. I will also engaged in several Exploratory Data Analysis to find out the factors that highly influence the set up of a restaurant within a given location.

Data Sources: Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nairobi_County (Nairobi County Data), FourSquare API: https://developer.foursquare.com/places-api 

Methodology

In my Analysis workflow, I majorly focused on Geographical Mapping and Analysing the connection between various geographical points. I first scraped a list of constituencies in Nairobi from this wikipedia link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nairobi_County. I then when on to look for the geographical coordinates of all these constituencies and added to my dataframe. This involve a considerable amount of data cleaning in cases of wrongly spelt constituencies etc. I was able to create a complete dataframe with (latitude, longitude) details of all the constituencies then proceeded to getting all the venues within 1km radius of the constituencies using the FourSquare API. With this data, I was able to map out all the “Restaurants, Resorts and Hotels in Nairobi County” which is visible here: {link to notebook}. From mapping the restaurants the relationships among the point were not clear so I decided to use the DBSCAN algorithm to cluster the points based on the distance between their geographical points. I successfully came up with 25 clusters of which based on my analysis the top 6 clusters were:

  • Cluster 1: Areas within Nairobi CBD and Parklands
  • Cluster 2: Kilimani Area
  • Cluster 3: Karen Area
  • Cluster 4: Nairobi West, South C Area
  • Cluster 5: Roysambu, Kasarani
  • Cluster 6: Embakasi Area

Cluster 1: Nairobi CBD and Parklands

Cluster 1, named Nairobi CBD and Parklands based on the frequency of Neighborhoods in the cluster is the most diverse of neighborhoods with 78 restaurants. Within this cluster Parklands contributes the most number of restaurants followed by Nairobi Central and Kileleshwa. This cluster has restaurants offering specific cuisines which speaks of the cultural diversity or international citizenship within the areas.

Cluster 2: Areas around KIlimani Area

Cluster 2, named Areas around Kilimani Area have a total of 30 restaurants, resorts and hotels. Even though the total number of restaurants in this cluster is small compared to the CBD/Parklands cluster there’s a considerable amount of diversity from the look of the unique hotels and restaurant categories based on the specific cuisines and international focus: i.e. African, Japanese, Indian, Brazilian etc.

Cluster 3: Karen Zone

Cluster 3, named Karen Zone is similar to Kilimani Cluster since there’s a considerable amount of diversity from the look of the unique hotels and restaurant categories based on the specific cuisines and international focus: i.e. African, Japanese, Indian, Brazilian etc. It is also noticeable that Karen has 2 thriving Fast Food restaurants alongside the Spanish, Indian, Japanese, Italian and Mediterranean restaurants. Karen Zone, cluster has a total of 10 restaurants.

Cluster 4: Nairobi Wests/South C Area

Cluster 4, named Nairobi West/South C Area has the highest number of Fast Food Restaurants i.e. 12 fast food restaurants. This is possibly due to high students population and the fact that Nairobi West is an area known for parties, clubbing and night social life. The cluster also has a Chinese and an Indian Restaurant which confirms the presence of an Indian community and Chinese Citizens in the area.

Cluster 5: Roysambu/Kasarani/Zimmerman Cluster

Cluster 5, named Roysambu/Kasarani Cluster has a total of 14 restaurants, resorts and hotels. Compared to the previous clusters, this cluster done not have any specific restaurant offering international cuisines. The area has 7 restaurants and the two popular hotels in Nairobi County i.e. Safari Park Hotel and Safari Park Hotel and Casino.

Discussion

From the analysis above I was able to Identify the following:

  • Most restaurants in Nairobi are located within the Central Business District and Constitute the most preferred restaurants by Delegates or Political Guests to Kenya. Areas within Nairobi Area have a total of 78 Restaurants/Hotels
  • Within Nairobi Central Business District, Karen area and Kilimani area: The number of international food serving restaurants are high, this is enough to give us some notion of population diversity within the area. It would therefore be best to launch a restaurant based on a given nationality within this areas e.g. Mexican, Spaniards, Indians, Japanese, Chinese, Ethiopian
  • Nairobi CBD and Parklands have the highest number of Restaurants Density ie. 78 in total which I feel is already clogged with competition therefore it would be best to venture into Karen area and Kilimani area with is still low compared to CBD/Parklands i.e. 30 and 10 respectively
  • Upcoming Areas for growth are Kasarani/Roysambu and Nairobi West/South C and Nairobi South area: Launching a Restaurant within this areas might prove to be a first mover advantage given the current growth in the number of international citizens within these areas.
